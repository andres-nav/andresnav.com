* General overview
Currently, I am pursuing a dual degree in Data Science and Telecommunication Engineering at the University Carlos III of Madrid, Spain.

This dual degree is a 5-year program that combines the technical skills of an engineer with the analytical skills of a data scientist.

It is a challenging program that requires a lot of dedication and hard work, but it is also rewarding.

In this article, I will share the strategies and habits that I have implemented to excel in university and earn top honors so that you can apply them to your own academic journey or life.

* Tips to Ace University
** 1. Focus Time is better than a Lot of Time
One of the biggest mistakes that I used to make was to study for long hours, thinking that the more time I spent studying, the better I would perform.

However, I realized that it is not about the quantity of time you spend studying but rather the quality of time.

Instead of studying for long hours, I focused on studying for short periods of time with high intensity and concentration.

I also applied this technique to other areas of my life, such as work and personal projects, and I noticed a significant improvement in my productivity and performance.

** 2. Understand, Connect, and Implement
Another mistake that I used to make was to memorize information without understanding it.

I realized that understanding the concepts and connecting them to real-life examples or applications is crucial to retaining the information and applying it in different contexts.

This is why I always try to understand the concepts, connect them to real-life examples or applications, and implement them in different contexts.

This approach has helped me not only to perform better in exams but also to develop a deeper understanding of the subjects and their practical applications.

** 3. Participation and Engagement
Participation and engagement are key to learning and retaining information.

I used to be a passive learner, attending classes without actively participating or engaging with the material.

However, I realized that active participation and engagement are crucial to learning and retaining information.

Try to always be present in class, ask questions, participate in discussions, and engage with the material.

And specially, do the labs and exercises, they are the best way to learn and understand the concepts.

** 4. Consistency and Discipline
If you are not able to do something for long periods of time without any external motivation, it will be really hard to achieve anything significant.

Consistency and discipline are key to achieving your goals and excelling in university.

For this, I found best to have a routine and stick to it.

For me, it is waking up early and working on my most important tasks first thing in the morning.

** 5. Own Your Mistakes
One of the biggest lessons that I have learned is that it is okay to make mistakes.

Mistakes are part of the learning process, and they are an opportunity to grow and improve.

However, it is important to own your mistakes, learn from them, and take responsibility for them.

Instead of blaming others or making excuses, take ownership of your mistakes, learn from them, and use them as an opportunity to grow and improve.

Mistakes are the hints of the places where you need to improve.

** 6. Learn and Be Curious
One of the best ways to excel in university and in life is to be curious and to never stop learning.

Always be curious, ask questions, seek answers, and never stop learning.

Try to find hobbies and interests around your field of study, this will help you to learn more and to be more motivated.

For example, in my case, I am really interested in designing and architecting software systems. So, I have learned about software architecture and design patterns, and I have applied them to my personal projects.

** 7. Results-Oriented Study
One of the biggest mistakes that I used to make was to study without a clear goal or objective.

It is best to take 5 minutes before starting anything and clarify what you want to achieve.

For example, before starting to study for an exam, clarify what you want to achieve, what topics you want to cover, and how you will measure your progress.

You are as good as the data that you measure.

** 8. Surround Yourself with High Achievers
Have you ever heard the saying, "You are the average of the five people you spend the most time with?"

This is true.

If you surround yourself with high achievers, you will be motivated to excel and to achieve your goals.

On the other hand, if you surround yourself with negative or unmotivated people, you will be demotivated and less likely to achieve your goals and be contempt with your current situation.

Surround yourself with high achievers, learn from them, and be motivated to excel and achieve your goals.


* Conclusion
In conclusion, excelling in university and earning top honors is not about innate intelligence or luck but rather about implementing effective strategies and habits.

By focusing on quality time, understanding, connecting, and implementing concepts, participating and engaging actively, being consistent and disciplined, owning your mistakes, being curious and never stop learning, being results-oriented, and surrounding yourself with high achievers, you can excel in university and achieve your goals.

I hope that you find these tips helpful and that you can apply them to your own academic journey or life.
